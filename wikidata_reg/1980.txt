In strict analysis, abbreviations should not be confused with or including initialisms with which they share some and functions, though all three are connoted by the term "abbreviation" in loose parlance <ref name harts> cite book title New Harts Rules: The handbook of style for writers and editors publisher Oxford University Press, 2005 id ISBN 0-19-861041-6 </ref> rp p167 However, normally acronyms are regarded as subgroup of abbreviations by the Council of Science Editors Examples are: OZ-Ounce, LBS-Pound, KILO-Kilogram, G-Gram
